Simpson.codes
Completion Date: January 2019
Skillset: Bootstrap CSS Git HTML Javascript JQuery

     This website is my own personal portfolio consisting of four main pages designed to illustrate who I am and what I have accomplished throughout my career in web development.

     My goal for creating this site was to place myself into the role of my targeted user (a recruiter or reviewer) and design a site to fit their needs. This resulted in what I see as two use cases: a 30 second visit and a 5 minute visit (or longer).

     The home page was designed to meet the requirements of a 30 second visit. My thought process was to place myself into the shoes of a reviewer who has a lot on their plate and has a short amount of time to give me, similar to a resume review process. As a result, I decided to only show a brief introduction about myself and my most recent projects on the home page. This shows the reviewer everything they would need to know in 30 seconds - who I am, and what I have done.

     The rest of the site was designed to be used during longer visits. It shows my work history and skill set (resume), more details on every project I have finished, and some more detailed information about myself. Each of these pages would be available if the reviewer wanted to know more about me, but would not be absolutely necessary.

     Overall, I think I accomplished my original goal, and I learned a lot in the process as well. I learned how to develop a github pages site, with the added bonus of learning how to use git to manage project check-ins. I improved on my web design skills, and overcame odd challenges such as having a form submission on a static website with no backend (I ended up using a Google form and submitting the form on my site to that). I believe this site represents me very well as of the time of completion and I look forward to being able to continue to improve myself as I finish more projects in the future.

CWS Co-Ops
Client: Campus Web Solutions
Completion Date: September 2018
Skillset: Bootstrap CSS C# HTML Javascript JQuery .NET MVC TFS

     In early spring 2018, myself and one other developer were tasked with redesigning the CWS website. During this redesign process I was responsible for developing the co-ops page, which shows potential co-op students the information they would need to know if they were to co-op at CWS, as well as a few components on the home page before handing those off to another developer.

     Our goal for the co-ops page was to display all important information cleanly and update our web design to 2018 standards. This was a challenge for me at the time due to having no prior resources to get me started. It was also the first website I had made using Bootstrap 4 so I had to learn it as I went as well.

In the end, I was able to develop a layout that looked pretty good and accomplished all of our goals. It utilized flexbox and the more full-screen/large font design practices of 2018 and taught me a lot about developing for the UX instead of just focusing on UI like I had in previous projects. Also, there were quite a few components I was very fond of, which is why you may notice some similarities between my portfolio site and this page.

AUFSTC (Requires AU Login)

Client: Auburn University SGA and Athletics
Completion Date: July 2017
Skillset: Bootstrap CSS C# Entity Framework HTML Javascript JQuery .NET MVC SQL TFS

     AUFSTC (Auburn University Football Student Ticketing Center) is by far the biggest and most high profile project I have ever created. It was designed to replace the old ticketing system the student body used to manage their football ticket purchases after SGA enacted new rules for football tickets prior to the 2017 season. AUFSTC allows all Auburn students to return their tickets to a central pool where other students may request from, manage their guest ticket purchases, manage any penalties that may occur, and appeal those penalties if they so desire.

     For this project, I was responsible for leading a team of myself and two other developers to create this project in a month and a half to ensure it would be ready for football season. My role as lead developer included ensuring the project was staying on schedule and presenting project updates before SGA and Auburn Athletics. My individual developer roles consisted of developing the entire site's UI/UX and included some backend work for form submissions throughout the site.

     After completion, the project was featured on the Auburn local televison station as well as in the campus paper. In the 2018 season alone, the ticket request page received over 32,000 hits and more than 1,600 penalty point appeals were made. This project was the biggest in my company's history at the time and is what I consider my greatest web achievement to date due to the high usage it receives and because of how important it is to the student body here at Auburn.
